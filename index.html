Analysing research

This looks very intimidating at first, but it's just very detailed. Tick items off as you do them so you don't forget!

    • Note which topic(s) the research talks about:
      • Market size, share, growth and competition
      • Market trends
      • Target market
    • type of source (primary, secondary, newspaper, research journal, etc.)
    • sample size - if known (a few people in a town? internet survey? data from a limited number of shops?)
    • strength, weakness, opportunity or threat for the business?
    • any link(s) to one or more of the 7 P's, if possible
      • price
      • product
      • place
      • promotion
      • people
      • processes
        • mainly for things like service industries (car garages, restaurants, plumbing, etc.)
      • physical environment
    • Example factors:
      • Unemployment rate
      • Inflation rate
        • Bank of England interest rate
      • Corporation tax
        • Current corporation tax rate
      • Minimum wage
        • Current minimum wage
      • Pollution and recycling
      • Taxes - import/export, VAT, Brexit costs
      • brief explanation of the factor
      • how it has changed over the last few years to now
      • (briefly) how it might affect the business
      • link to research
      • Competitor pricing
      • Competitor promotions
      • Competitor sales figures
      • Competitor market share
      • Explanation of the factor
      • How it affects the business
      • How it affects the marketing campaign (price/product/place/promotion)
      • What research backs it up?

Part B - work in progress

You won't have access to this resource during the 3 hours of Part B.

Your notes created in Part A CANNOT contain any pre-made marketing plans, Part B structure or significant data analysis.

Write-up

Section 1

  • Write up 3 chunky paragraphs on: market share/size/growth/competition; market trends; target market
    • what does the data show?
    • why is this an strength/weakness/opportunity/threat?
    • what research data supports this?
    • what might this s/w/o/t lead to?
    • how could it influence the marketing campaign? (optional, but preferred)
      • price
      • product
      • place
      • promotion
      • people
      • processes
      • physical environment
    • is the data reliable?
      • where does the data come from? is it independent and reliable?
      • is the data up-to-date?
      • is the research appropriate for the target market?
      • is the sample size big enough?
  • Write up SWOT
    • Internal environment
      • Strength
      • Weakness
    • External environment
      • Opportunity
        • From PESTLE factor(s)
          • Social factors (e.g., market trends)
          • Environmental factors (e.g., climate change)
      • Threat
        • From PESTLE factor(s)
          • Economic factors (e.g., inflation)

Section 2

  • SMART objectives x3
    • One each about...
      • product
      • price
      • promotion
    • Use JAM structure
      • Why is it important for the business? Link to their aim.
      • How does this objective link to the case study?
      • What might it lead to or allow?
      • How does the objective relate to the the provided research about the market?
  • Marketing message
    • Explain what is for sale in a memorable way
    • Show customer wishes are being listened to and addressed
    • The problem solved for the customer
    • What makes you different
    • Why the customer should trust you
  • 7P's about your marketing plan - small paragraph for each (JAM)
  • Budget & timescale
    • Justify your decisions
